The 2019 novel coronavirus, or COVID-19, is a new respiratory virus first identified in Wuhan, China. Symptoms of COVID-19 may include fever, cough, and shortness of breath. The outbreak has been detected in more than 100 locations internationally, including in the United States. The COVID-19 outbreak has been characterized as a pandemic by the World Health Organization (WHO). Learn about 2019 novel coronavirus from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
